name: Prova marketing positioning rules
description: Hard rules for Prova marketing copy — agentic acquiring framing, no framework names, no fabricated metrics
type: feedback
---

When writing marketing copy for Prova (the agentic merchant acquiring platform):

**Never mention the agent framework by name.** Mastra (or whatever framework Prova uses) is an implementation detail. The brand is "agentic acquiring platform" — the framework is irrelevant to the buyer.

**Why:** Banks/ISOs don't care which framework runs underneath; naming it makes Prova sound like a wrapper rather than a platform. Treat framework choice the same way Stripe treats Go vs. Ruby — invisible.
**How to apply:** No "powered by X", no SDK name-drops, no framework links. If devs need to know, it goes in technical docs only.

---

**Never invent quantitative claims about labor savings or staffing ratios.** Specifically: do not say things like "run a 10K merchant portfolio with 100 employees" — ISOs already run extremely lean (often <10 staff for thousands of merchants), so labor-reduction framing both insults the buyer and is factually wrong.

**Why:** The buyer is the ISO/bank exec who *knows* the real ratios. Made-up labor numbers destroy credibility instantly. Acquiring is already a thin-margin lean-staff business — the pitch isn't "fewer humans," it's better visibility and consistency.
**How to apply:** No fabricated percentages, no fabricated speed claims, no headcount comparisons. Quant claims must be defensible (e.g., real benchmark numbers, not aspirational).

---

**The Prova differentiator is unparalleled visibility across the bank → ISO → merchant chain, not labor savings.** Frame everything around traceability, replay, transparency, and shared truth between parties — not "agents replacing analysts."

**Why:** Today every party in the acquiring chain sees a fragment. Banks can't see what their ISOs are doing. ISOs can't see what merchants are experiencing. Merchants can't see why their hold exists. An agentic platform makes every action traceable and auditable end-to-end. *That's* the wedge — not headcount.
**How to apply:** Lead with visibility/observability/traceability language. Use "end to end from bank to merchant" framing. Agents are how the visibility happens, not the headline.
