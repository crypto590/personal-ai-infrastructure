# a16z Pitch Research

## Investment Thesis — What a16z Looks For

### Marc Andreessen's Product-Market Fit Framework
- "Product/market fit means being in a good market with a product that can satisfy that market"
- Market matters most: "In a great market — a market with lots of real potential customers — the market pulls product out of the startup"
- PMF signs: usage growing faster than you can support, money piling up, hiring as fast as possible
- No-PMF signs: customers aren't getting value, word of mouth isn't spreading, reviews are meh

### a16z Evaluation Criteria
1. **Market size** — must be large or rapidly growing
2. **Product differentiation** — 10x better on a key dimension
3. **Team** — deep domain expertise + ability to execute
4. **Technical moat** — proprietary technology, data, or insight
5. **Business model scalability** — unit economics that improve with scale

## The a16z Pitch Structure

a16z partners have described what they want to see:

1. **What you do** (one sentence)
2. **Problem** — with evidence it's real and painful
3. **Solution** — your product, demonstrated
4. **Why now** — what changed (technology, regulation, behavior)
5. **Market** — bottom-up TAM with credible expansion path
6. **Traction** — metrics that prove demand (revenue > users > signups)
7. **Business model** — unit economics, pricing, LTV/CAC
8. **Competition** — honest positioning, what you do better
9. **Team** — why this team wins in this market
10. **The ask** — amount, use of funds, milestones

## Key Unit Economics (a16z benchmarks)

- **LTV/CAC ratio:** 3:1 minimum, 4:1+ preferred
- **CAC payback:** <18 months consumer, <24 months B2B
- **Gross margin:** 70%+ for SaaS
- **Net Revenue Retention:** >100% = expansion within accounts

## What a16z Says NO To

1. Solution in search of a problem
2. Markets that aren't big enough for venture-scale returns
3. "Better UX" as the sole differentiator — not a moat
4. No evidence of customer pull
5. Teams without domain expertise in complex markets
6. Business models that don't scale (services disguised as software)

## Ben Horowitz Insights (The Hard Thing About Hard Things)

- Lead with honesty, not optimism. Investors respect founders who name the hard parts.
- "There is no secret to being a CEO. Sadly there is no trick, no shortcut."
- The pitch should reveal how you think under pressure, not how polished your slides are.

## Stage Differences

| Stage | What They Need to See |
|-------|----------------------|
| Seed | Vision + team + early signal of demand |
| Series A | PMF evidence + repeatable growth + unit economics |
| Series B+ | Scalable machine + defensible position + category leadership |
